Wildfire Information & Preparedness Tips

What is a Wildfire?

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire that rapidly spreads across vegetation and forest areas. Wildfires can cause extensive damage to ecosystems, property, and can threaten human lives.

Understanding how to prepare for and respond to wildfires is crucial for minimizing risks and protecting your community.

Preparedness Tips

  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing dead plants, leaves, and other flammable materials.
  • Keep a hose and fire extinguishers ready in case of an emergency.
  • Ensure your home has fire-resistant roofing and siding materials.
  • Plan multiple evacuation routes and practice them with your family.
  • Close all windows, doors, and vents during a wildfire to prevent embers from entering your home.
  • Stay informed about local wildfire risks and alerts through reliable sources.

Protection Acts

  • Wildfire Disaster Recovery Act: Provides resources for recovery and rebuilding efforts after a wildfire.
  • Fire Management Assistance Act: Assists local governments with wildfire suppression and firefighting efforts.
  • National Fire Plan: Supports wildfire prevention, management, and restoration activities across the country.
  • Community Wildfire Protection Plan: Encourages communities to develop and implement plans to reduce wildfire risks.

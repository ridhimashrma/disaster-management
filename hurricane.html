Hurricane Information & Preparedness Tips

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a type of tropical cyclone, which is a rotating low-pressure weather system that has organized thunderstorms but no fronts.

Hurricanes can cause devastating damage through high winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges. It is critical to be well-prepared and stay informed during hurricane season.

Preparedness Tips

  • Know your evacuation routes and have a plan in place in case you need to leave your home.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, medications, and important documents.
  • Reinforce windows and doors with storm shutters or plywood to protect against high winds.
  • Stay informed through reliable sources about hurricane warnings and updates in your area.
  • Secure outdoor objects like furniture and grills to prevent them from becoming projectiles during a storm.
  • Move valuable items to higher levels of your home to protect them from potential flooding.

Protection Acts

  • National Hurricane Program: Coordinates federal, state, and local efforts in hurricane preparedness, response, and recovery.
  • Hurricane Preparedness Act: Provides resources and guidance for communities to better prepare for and respond to hurricanes.
  • Disaster Mitigation Act: Aims to reduce the impact of natural disasters, including hurricanes, through comprehensive mitigation planning.
  • Emergency Management Assistance Compact: Facilitates state-to-state assistance during disasters, ensuring swift and effective response efforts.

Earthquake Information & Preparedness Tips

What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is the shaking of the surface of the Earth resulting from a sudden release of energy in the Earth's lithosphere that creates seismic waves.

Earthquakes can cause damage to buildings, infrastructure, and result in loss of life. It is important to be informed and prepared for such events.

Preparedness Tips

  • Secure heavy furniture and objects that could fall during an earthquake.
  • Have an emergency kit ready with food, water, and medical supplies.
  • Know the safe spots in your home (under sturdy furniture, away from windows).
  • Practice “Drop, Cover, and Hold On” with your family and co-workers.
  • Ensure your building is earthquake-resistant and complies with safety standards.
  • Stay informed through reliable sources about earthquake risks in your area.

Protection Acts

  • Earthquake Hazard Reduction Act: Mandates the implementation of safety measures in buildings to reduce earthquake risks.
  • Seismic Safety Standards Act: Establishes guidelines for earthquake-resistant construction and retrofitting.
  • Emergency Response Act: Ensures that disaster response plans are in place and regularly updated for effective emergency management.
  • Community Resilience Act: Promotes community-based initiatives to enhance preparedness and recovery from earthquakes.

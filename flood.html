Flood Information & Preparedness Tips

What is a Flood?

A flood is an overflow of water that submerges land that is usually dry. It can result from heavy rain, river overflow, or other factors.

Floods can cause extensive damage to property and infrastructure, as well as pose serious risks to life. Being informed and prepared is crucial.

Preparedness Tips

  • Identify flood-prone areas near your location and plan safe evacuation routes.
  • Elevate critical utilities (electrical panels, sockets, wiring) and appliances above potential flood levels.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, medications, and important documents.
  • Install check valves in plumbing to prevent floodwater from backing up into your home.
  • Stay informed through reliable sources about weather alerts and flood warnings in your area.
  • Consider purchasing flood insurance to protect your property from potential damage.

Protection Acts

  • National Flood Insurance Act: Provides financial protection against flood-related property damage.
  • Flood Disaster Protection Act: Requires the purchase of flood insurance for properties in flood-prone areas.
  • Coastal Barrier Resources Act: Restricts federal funding for development in designated coastal barrier regions to reduce flood risks.
  • Community Rating System: Encourages community-level floodplain management activities to lower flood insurance premiums.

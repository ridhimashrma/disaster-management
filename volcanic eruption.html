Volcanic Eruption Information & Preparedness Tips

What is a Volcanic Eruption?

A volcanic eruption occurs when magma from beneath the Earth's crust escapes to the surface, often causing explosive releases of gas, ash, and lava. Volcanic eruptions can cause widespread damage and pose significant risks to life and property.

Understanding volcanic activity and being prepared can significantly reduce the risks associated with eruptions.

Preparedness Tips

  • Know the evacuation routes and plan ahead with your family.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like masks, food, water, and first aid supplies.
  • Keep your vehicle fueled and ready for evacuation.
  • Monitor volcanic alerts and advisories from reliable sources.
  • Stay indoors during ashfall, and close windows and doors to avoid inhaling ash.
  • Protect livestock and pets by bringing them indoors or ensuring they have adequate shelter.

Protection Acts

  • The Volcanic Hazards Mitigation Act: Establishes guidelines for monitoring and responding to volcanic activity.
  • The Evacuation and Safety Act: Provides resources for planning and executing safe evacuations in volcanic regions.
  • The Volcano Monitoring Initiative: Supports the installation and maintenance of monitoring equipment around active volcanoes.
  • The Disaster Recovery Act: Offers aid and support for communities affected by volcanic eruptions.

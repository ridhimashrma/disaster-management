Drought Information & Preparedness Tips

What is a Drought?

A drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water. Droughts can have severe impacts on agriculture, water supply, and the environment.

Being prepared for drought conditions is essential to mitigate the effects on communities and the environment.

Preparedness Tips

  • Conserve water by fixing leaks and using water-efficient appliances.
  • Use drought-resistant plants in your garden to reduce water usage.
  • Store water in rain barrels for gardening and other non-potable uses.
  • Avoid outdoor water use during the hottest part of the day to minimize evaporation.
  • Protect your property by installing soil erosion controls and maintaining healthy soil.
  • Stay informed about local water restrictions and drought conditions in your area.

Protection Acts

  • National Drought Mitigation Act: Provides resources and guidelines for drought monitoring, planning, and response.
  • Water Conservation Act: Establishes policies and incentives to encourage water conservation efforts during drought conditions.
  • Drought Preparedness Act: Supports state and local governments in developing drought preparedness and response plans.
  • Emergency Water Management Act: Facilitates coordinated efforts to manage water resources during drought emergencies.

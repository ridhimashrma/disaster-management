Heatwave Information & Prevention Tips

What is a Heatwave?

A heatwave is a prolonged period of excessively hot weather, which may be accompanied by high humidity. Heatwaves can lead to heat-related illnesses and stress on water and energy supplies.

Being informed and prepared can help mitigate the effects of heatwaves and protect your health and safety.

Prevention Tips

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and avoid sugary or alcoholic beverages.
  • Keep your home cool by using fans, air conditioning, and closing curtains or blinds during the day.
  • Limit outdoor activities during peak heat hours, usually between 10 AM and 4 PM.
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored, and loose-fitting clothing, and use sunscreen to protect your skin.
  • Check on elderly neighbors, young children, and pets, as they are more vulnerable to heat-related illnesses.
  • Stay informed about heatwave alerts and advisories through reliable sources.

Protection Acts

  • The Heat Emergency Act: Requires the development of heat emergency plans and provides resources for vulnerable populations.
  • The Climate Adaptation Act: Encourages cities to implement measures to reduce the urban heat island effect and improve resilience to heatwaves.
  • The Renewable Energy Act: Promotes the use of renewable energy sources to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate the impact of heatwaves.
  • The Urban Greenery Initiative: Supports the planting of trees and creation of green spaces in urban areas to reduce heat and improve air quality.
